Assumptions

This tool estimates EV charging costs by applying your blended electricity rate to annual kilowatt-hour consumption. It does not simulate individual charging sessions.

  • All charging is at home — no public or fast-charger usage is factored in. If you do a portion of your driving on road trips, reduce the annual distance proportionally.
  • All charging lands in your chosen windows — if you select "All Overnight", the model assumes 100% of your annual kWh draw happens between 11pm–6am with no spillover.
  • Linear consumption — annual kWh is simply kWh/100km × annual km. Whether that's 10 kWh every night or 60 kWh twice a week makes no difference to the total.
  • No charging losses — a real 7 kW wall charger is ~90–95% efficient, so actual grid draw would be 5–10% higher than what reaches the battery. This applies equally to all plans, so relative comparisons hold.
  • No battery degradation — consumption per km is treated as constant over the year.
  • Battery capacity is irrelevant — whether your EV has a 40 kWh or 80 kWh pack, only total annual kWh consumed matters for cost.
  • Rates are effective 1 July 2025 — Synergy typically adjusts tariffs annually. Future rate changes are not modelled.

These assumptions apply equally across all three plans, so the relative savings remain accurate regardless of individual charging patterns.
